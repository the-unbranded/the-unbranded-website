MISSION

The Unbranded seeks to humanize the process of professional branding while also establishing a reproducible, transparent, and ethically sound business structure. We pride ourselves in tailoring our work to reflect our clients' unique perspectives, while striving to consistently challenge ourselves and our clients to integrate an ethos of positive change into every action taken, no matter how big or small.

ABOUT

We are a web presence cooperative - a diverse* group of creatives that are passionate about collaborating with like-minded people to design authentic digital footprints that drive growth. As a collective of creators exploring new skills, we seek to work with fellow artists who are also on the path to establishing their portfolios and brand identities.

The Unbranded was formed from a group of innovators who share a mindset of cooperation, ethical business practices, and individualized, personal attention to client needs.

While we are still building our client base and skillset, we offer expert empathy, passion, and upfront flat rates. A vital aspect of our mission is to offer pro-bono services to marginalized business owners. We offer: Website Development and Design | Visual Design | Copywriting | Brand Cohesion.

*A note on diversity - we are proud of our current self-selecting team of four women but fully acknowledge that there is more diversity to be incorporated. We look forward to increasing team diversity as we grow and are focusing on a diverse client base in the meantime.

SOCIAL JUSTICE

Social justice is an integral value at The Unbranded. We believe that ethical business practices and creativity should go hand in hand, and we are dedicated to working with clients and collaborators who believe in creating equity in representation at every level of business. We are committed to removing the gatekeeping of professional marketing services and making them accessible to all creative business owners. Our rate structure and pro-bono work will reflect a sustained dedication to taking on clients from traditionally marginalized groups, and we are always looking for new ways to incorporate positive social justice change into the everyday workings of the business.

Below you will find links to groups that co-owners currently feel need a spotlight. These groups have been vetted as far as any individual could vet a non-profit; we do not have resources beyond what an individual is capable of researching and we reserve the right to rescind links as new information surfaces. As with everything, this is a learning process, and admitting and learning from mistakes is important. As often as possible, if an organization is removed from our links because of a change of heart, we will share that information on our blog. Many links will be changed due to the team wanting to highlight more pressing issues, and links that we continue to support can be found on our Social Justice Archive page.

We strive to maintain a 4:1 paid to pro-bono project ratio, and have from our inception.

THE TEAM

SARAH BELING

Founder Advisor

Copywriting

Enamored at a young age with storytelling, Sarah tortured her family and friends throughout the 90s with re-worked revivals of classics like The Wizard of Oz and Annie (in which she gracefully accepted the title role). Sarah eventually moved to New York, attended NYU Tisch to study acting, threw out all of her clothing that wasn’t black, and developed a taste for bodega breakfast sandwiches. Sarah writes plays that range from thriller to 80s musical parody and is eager to help others tell their stories.

Jennifer Cameron

Web Development

A reluctant "techy," Jennifer started life with a strong emphasis on the arts: painting, acting, dance, and drawing (including an unfortunate incident involving a sharpie and her parent's carpet). However, a strong desire and affinity for logic and tech activities flowed though all aspects of life, school and work until she could no longer deny the pull and jumped into the world of web development. Jennifer now focuses on feeding both parts of her soul by creating things that are pleasing and functional.

AMELITA LIJEK

Founder

Business Ops and Design

Amelita has always had an instinct for management and organization. Beginning her executive career at age four when she dutifully (and, in retrospect, likely annoyingly) pointed out who was off of their line in ballet class, she has honed that intuition through a wide variety of jobs – working in everything from retail and fine dining to musical comedy and film production, with pit stops along the way to pick up some dog training skills and an undergrad degree from NYU. Her non-traditional path (though stressful for her parents) has taught her that following her gut when it comes to ethical values is essential and that this principle too often gets lost within workplaces. While pivoting to coding and design in the chaos of 2020, the idea for The Unbranded presented itself as an opportunity to constructively exercise her management skills, and more importantly put her values into practice. She is incredibly grateful and excited to get to do both with such a remarkable team beside her.

HEATHER RANDOLPH
